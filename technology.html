SAN FRANCISCO — Technology Desk, 09:00 UTC

Grok 4.5's first independent benchmarks land it in fourth place, behind Claude and GPT-5.5

A day after Elon Musk claimed Grok 4.5 had landed at "number one on multiple benchmarks," independent testing from Artificial Analysis ranked it fourth on its Intelligence Index, behind Claude Fable 5, GPT-5.5 and Claude Opus 4.8. xAI's model did post a strong result on the SWE marathon, a long-horizon coding benchmark, which analysts linked to Cursor session data gathered during xAI's SpaceX-era training runs.

    OpenAI and Anthropic both ship "agentic work" platforms in the same 24 hours

    OpenAI merged its Codex coding tool into ChatGPT to form a single application while launching ChatGPT Work, and Anthropic responded on the same day with Claude Cowork for web and mobile, removing the previous requirement that agentic sessions stay tethered to a desktop.

  • MOUNTAIN VIEW — 07:30 UTC

    Gemini 3.5 Pro remains stuck in preview, now six weeks past its target launch

    Google's Gemini 3.5 Pro has no confirmed public launch date and remains limited to an enterprise preview on Vertex AI, six weeks past its original GA target and five weeks past its I/O commitment, with no new timeline issued even as rivals GPT-5.6 and Grok 4.5 have both shipped.

    Meta has begun construction on a large new AI data center in Canada, part of a broader wave of infrastructure spending across the industry that also includes Europe's push for sovereign access to advanced AI systems and fresh efforts to catalog AI jailbreak vulnerabilities like conventional security flaws.

    Apple's Mac line grows even as the broader PC market shrinks for the first time in years

    Apple was the only major PC vendor to post significant shipment growth in the second quarter, according to IDC data, even as the wider PC market contracted for the first time in more than two years amid rising memory costs tied to AI-driven demand.
