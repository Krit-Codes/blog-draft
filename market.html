Chips carry the tape

Global equities shrugged off another flare-up between Washington and Tehran this week, with semiconductor names doing the heavy lifting. Five dispatches from the trading floor, filed Friday morning.

NEW YORK — Markets Desk, 06:00 UTC

Asian shares rally and Wall Street shrugs off Iran flare-up as AI trade regains momentum

MSCI's Asia Pacific equities gauge rose 1.2% and Hong Kong's Hang Seng climbed 1.2%, heading for its best week in more than a year, while South Korea's Kospi jumped 4% as SK Hynix priced a $26.5 billion American depositary share offering. On Wall Street, the Nasdaq Composite finished 1.3% higher and the S&P 500 gained 0.8%, even as futures wobbled overnight on lingering unease over the Strait of Hormuz.

Bloomberg
  • CHICAGO — 05:15 UTC

    Dow, S&P and Nasdaq all close higher despite fresh US–Iran strikes

    The Dow Jones Industrial Average added 0.3% to close at 52,478.41, the Nasdaq rose 1.3% to 26,206.89 and the S&P 500 gained 0.8% to 7,543.64, as falling crude prices offset renewed hostilities in the Middle East and jobless claims came in below forecasts.

    Zacks Investment Research / Yahoo Finance
  • MENLO PARK — 04:40 UTC

    Meta and Micron shares jump on custom AI chip and platform news

    Meta said it will begin manufacturing a customized AI chip from September, aiming to lift its computing power to 14 gigawatts by 2027, and unveiled an update to its Muse Spark AI platform that it billed as a direct challenge to rivals. Micron and Meta shares advanced 4.5% and 4.7% respectively on the news.

    Zacks Investment Research
  • SEOUL — 03:10 UTC

    SK Hynix set for Nasdaq debut after heavily oversubscribed US listing

    The South Korean memory-chip giant's US share offering was reported to be more than seven times oversubscribed, underscoring investor appetite for AI-linked hardware makers. Fellow flash-memory producer Kioxia also rallied as much as 11% after Bain Capital sold its entire stake in the company.

    CNBC
  • NEW YORK — 02:20 UTC

    Polymarket traders bet on a weaker S&P 500 open despite the week's rally

    A Polymarket contract implied just a 20% chance the S&P 500 would open higher Friday, even after Thursday's rebound, as traders weighed easing oil prices against persistent geopolitical risk and a possible Federal Reserve rate move later this year. Oracle, meanwhile, was downgraded by S&P Global Ratings amid concerns over its cash flow.
