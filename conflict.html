Day 132

The US–Iran ceasefire that briefly held this spring is fraying again. Five dispatches tracking the strikes, the retaliation, and the diplomacy still limping along beneath them.

WASHINGTON / TEHRAN — Conflict Desk, 08:00 UTC

Fragile US–Iran ceasefire appears to collapse as both sides trade new strikes

US Central Command said its forces struck approximately 90 Iranian military targets in a fresh round of attacks, days after President Trump declared the ceasefire "over" following Iranian strikes on shipping in the Strait of Hormuz. Iran's government says at least 14 people were killed in the latest US strikes, while explosions were also reported in the Iranian cities of Konarak and Bushehr, near a nuclear power plant and a naval zone.

CBS News / CNN
  • AMMAN — 07:20 UTC

    Iran strikes Gulf states in retaliation; Jordan intercepts missiles overhead

    Iran's military said it launched attack drones at sites in Kuwait, Qatar and Bahrain in response to earlier US strikes, and separately targeted a base in Jordan housing American forces. Jordan's armed forces said they intercepted several missiles and drones entering its airspace, insisting the kingdom will not let its territory be used by either side in the conflict.

    RFE/RL
  • MASHHAD — 06:10 UTC

    Iran buries Supreme Leader Khamenei as successor stays out of public view

    Iran's slain Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was buried following days of funeral ceremonies at the Imam Reza shrine. His son and successor, Mojtaba Khamenei, has not made a public appearance since assuming the country's highest political and religious office, fueling speculation about the transition.

    CNN
  • DOHA — 05:00 UTC

    Diplomacy limps on: technical nuclear talks continue despite the fighting

    A US official said Washington remains committed to negotiations with Tehran and that technical talks are ongoing even as strikes continue, with Qatar and Pakistan among the nations working to restart broader negotiations between the two governments.

    Al Jazeera
  • DUBAI — 03:40 UTC

    Strait of Hormuz disruptions ripple through global shipping and oil

    The conflict has forced shipping reroutes away from the Strait of Hormuz and the Red Sea and halted flights in and out of the region, after Iran fired on commercial vessels in the strait earlier in the week — a waterway that carries roughly a fifth of the world's oil consumption.

    Britannica / Gulf News
